Air Quality Index (AQI)

The AQI is a standardized measurement used by environmental agencies to communicate air quality to the public. It takes into account various pollutants, including ozone, particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide, and assigns a numerical value representing the level of pollution.
